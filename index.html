Interest In IT

I really love computers and all things that have to do with computers or Information Technology. My earliest memory of my abilities with IT was probably when I was 3 years old and I was at my grandfather’s house. I asked him if I could watch a specific VHS tape, he said that he did not know how to use it and wouldn’t able to help me. So, I responded, “It’s okay I will figure it out” and was able to plug in and start watching my VHS tape he was shell-shocked to say the least.
Later on, in life I was always the go to person for my friends with setting up their computer or helping them preform basic actions in the school computer lab. I always have had an unexplainable sense of excitement whenever I am able to fix an IT problem. I don’t have any professional IT experience however I find that I am able to perform problem solving solutions when it comes to IT with ease. My excitement for IT probably started when I took apart a computer in Year 9 Cyberspace class and got to see the RAM, CPU, GPU etc. in all its glory, was a pretty cool moment.
I chose to come to RMIT as I personally did not really enjoy the learning structure of school, Monash would’ve been my first preference at the beginning of Year 12 as it is close to home and is where most of my friends are. However I came to realise that the learning style of Monash would be far too similar to school and I really want to go somewhere that I enjoy, I had heard RMIT would be much more of a relaxed, hands on learning environment and so far it has met all my expectations. During my studies I would hope to be very proficient in programming and be able to develop my own software and understand all the elements of IT such as networking and servers.

The results seem to indicate a common trend of extraversion being my strongest trait. Being an extrovert in the IT field seems to be a unique trait which will really help me in achieving goals and engaging with clients.

My openness in the big five personality test and my prospecting trait in the Morrisby-Briggs test will help me work within a team environment as I will be able to hear different ideas and can implement actions for decision making and planning. When forming a team I should take these negative traits into account, as to find solutions to combat them.
On the positive side I should embrace my extraversion by taking initiative in the team environment and building on ideas and motivating others.
